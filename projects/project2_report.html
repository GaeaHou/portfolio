AGAINST the Proposition

  • Omitted exact gap values between bars in the visualization, potentially causing readers to misinterpret the real magnitude of disparity.
  • Selected only extreme examples (top 5 largest gaps) without showing overall global context, possibly exaggerating perceived inequalities.

Final Reflection

Through this project, I learned that even subtle visualization design choices can strongly influence interpretation. By adjusting color schemes, scale ranges, or focus selection, one can either emphasize real patterns or distort viewer perception. Especially on socially sensitive topics like gender equity in education, it is crucial to design visualizations responsibly, ensuring clarity, fairness, and minimizing bias. This project deepened my awareness of the persuasive and deceptive potential inherent in all visual communication.
